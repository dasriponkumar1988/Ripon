    <h1>All Divisions Information of Bangladesh</h1>
    <p>Bangladesh is divided into 8 major administrative divisions. Each division is named after the major city within its jurisdiction that also serves as the administrative headquarters of that division.</p>
    
    <hr>

    <h2>1. Dhaka Division</h2>
    <center><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%A2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%95%E0%A6%BE_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg/250px-%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%A2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%95%E0%A6%BE_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg.png" alt="Dhaka Division Map" width="300"> </center>
    <p>Dhaka Division is the Capital City of Bangladesh, serving as the nation’s political, cultural, and economic epicenter. It is the most populous administrative region in the country. <strong>Visiting sites:</strong> Lalbagh Fort and Ahsan Manzil. <strong>Historical Place:</strong> Bangladesh National Museum. <strong>Famous Food:</strong> Old Dhaka Biryani & Bakarkhani.</p>
    <hr>

    <h2>2. Chattogram Division</h2>
    <center><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Chattogram_in_Bangladesh.svg/330px-Chattogram_in_Bangladesh.svg.png" alt="Chattogram Division Map" width="300"></center>
    <p>Chattogram Division is the southeastern gateway of Bangladesh. It is the largest division by area. <strong>Visiting site : </strong> Cox’s Bazar Sea Beach – longest natural sea beach in the world. <strong>Historical Place:</strong> Ethnological Museum. <strong>Famous Food:</strong> Mezban beef.</p>
    <hr>

    <h2>3. Rajshahi Division</h2>
    <center><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/74/%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%B0%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%9C%E0%A6%B6%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%B9%E0%A7%80_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg/250px-%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%B0%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%9C%E0%A6%B6%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%B9%E0%A7%80_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg.png" alt="Rajshahi Division Map" width="300"></center>
    <p>Known as the "Silk City" and the "Mango Capital," Rajshahi is located in the northwest. <strong>Visiting site : </strong> Mahasthangarh – ancient archaeological site. <strong>Historical Place:</strong> Puthia Temple Complex – terracotta Hindu temples. <strong>Famous Food:</strong> Rajshahi mangoes.</p>
    <hr>

    <h2>4. Khulna Division</h2>
    <center><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b9/%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%96%E0%A7%81%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%A8%E0%A6%BE_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg/250px-%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%96%E0%A7%81%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%A8%E0%A6%BE_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg.png" alt="Khulna Division Map" width="300"></center>
    <p>Khulna Division is home to the <strong>Sundarbans</strong>, the world’s largest mangrove forest and habitat of the Royal Bengal Tiger. <strong>Visiting site : </strong> Sundarbans Mangrove Forest – world’s largest mangrove. <strong>Historical Place:</strong> Sixty Dome Mosque (Shat Gambuj Masjid) – historic Muslim architecture. <strong>Famous Food:</strong> Local fish & regional pithas (Bangla sweets).</p>
    <hr>

    <h2>5. Sylhet Division</h2>
    <center><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%B8%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%B2%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%9F_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg/250px-%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%B8%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%B2%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%9F_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg.png" alt="Sylhet Division Map" width="300"></center>
    <p>Known as the <strong>"Land of 360 Saints,"</strong> Sylhet is famous for its lush tea gardens and spiritual significance. <strong>Visiting site : </strong> Jaflong – scenic river valley & hills. <strong>Historical Place:</strong> Mosque & shrine sites in Sylhet Sadar (e.g., Hazrat Shah Jalal Mazar). <strong>Famous Food:</strong> Tea & citrus fruits common in Sylhet.</p>
    <hr>

    <h2>6. Barishal Division</h2>
    <center><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8b/%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%B0%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%B6%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%B2_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg/250px-%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%B0%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%B6%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%B2_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg.png" alt="Barishal Division Map" width="300"></center>
    <p>Barishal is a riverine maze in the south-central delta, historically known as the <strong> "Granary of Bengal."</strong> <strong> Visiting site : </strong> Kuakata Sea Beach – panoramic sea & sunrise/sunset views. <strong>Historical Place:</strong> River port heritage sites in Barishal city. <strong>Famous Food:</strong> Guava & local river fish delicacies. </p>
    <hr>

    <h2>7. Rangpur Division</h2>
    <center><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Rangpur_in_Bangladesh.svg/250px-Rangpur_in_Bangladesh.svg.png" alt="Rangpur Division Map" width="300"></center>
    <p>Rangpur Division forms the northern tip of Bangladesh. It is an agricultural hub known for tobacco, maize, and coal mines. <strong> Visiting site : </strong> Kantanagar Temple – historic Hindu temple. <strong>Historical Place:</strong> Tajhat Palace – colonial period palace museum. <strong>Famous Food:</strong> Local sweets & agricultural produce (e.g., shingara).</p>
    <hr>

    <h2>8. Mymensingh Division</h2>
    <center><img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%AE%E0%A6%AF%E0%A6%BC%E0%A6%AE%E0%A6%A8%E0%A6%B8%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B9_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg/250px-%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B2%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%A6%E0%A7%87%E0%A6%B6%E0%A7%87_%E0%A6%AE%E0%A6%AF%E0%A6%BC%E0%A6%AE%E0%A6%A8%E0%A6%B8%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%82%E0%A6%B9_%E0%A6%AC%E0%A6%BF%E0%A6%AD%E0%A6%BE%E0%A6%97.svg.png" alt="Mymensingh Division Map" width="300"></center>
    <p>The youngest division in Bangladesh, Mymensingh is a land of rich folk culture. It is the nation's leader in freshwater fish production. Landmarks include the 19th-century <strong>Shashi Lodge</strong> and <strong>Alexander Castle</strong>, also known as the "Iron House."</p>
